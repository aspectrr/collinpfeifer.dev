## On Writing

I think it is easily one of the most useufl tools that we can use as humans to protray and extract our thoughts from the swirling whirlpools of our minds and lay them out lceanly into a medium that does not reward abstracts or misinterpretations, but pure clean thoughts.

Writing forces you to extract the logical thread between your ideas and to not be wrong. If you are wrong or were lying to yourself, writing will show it to you.

It is one long formal proof (i would rather say philisophical proof, but can be mathematical in a way), to explain an idea. When you come along a misstep or a fault, that is where the insights come. Have yo been crayz this entire time? Or do you have the answer to cross the cashm? maybe you don't know as well which is totally fine. Time to do research and figure it out.


I think it is a level of artistic expression as well. You are forced to explain the things that you are thinking which can be very helpful.

Could there be a possible danger as well? Like how Bezos describes initial ideas like acorns and how if we are not careful we can kill them? Does writing help acrons grow or do we need the partly ambigious lying mind to help them grow? Do we need that level of misrepresentation to get started?

Either way it is a great filter. You get as close to reality as possible and I also think it is why writing has been preserved as a medium for millenia. You get to have ideas preserved through words that can be trasnferred across generations, people, time. These things are translucent. Far beyond whatever we can understand in one lifetime.
