I am not that smart. I don't think of myself as very smart. I enjoy learning new things and I enjoy learning deeply at my own pace. Couple reasons.

1. I don't like making assumptions. In school I would ask a lot of questions and after a while I got tired of asking. I didn't like making assumptions about the things that we were learning and the priors needed for them and so I would ask questions to learn more and negate any priors that I didn't know. However I now believe that schools make lots of assumptions about things and if you don't know them then you will be left.

2. I like deeply understanding things. It's why I love LLMs so much, I can do as deep as I want with as much previous knowledge as I need to understand something, quite remarkable. In school I would hold up the whole class. Intelligence and the way it was taught to me is rate of learning. The faster you can shove new nodes in your brain, the smarter you are. I like to deeply understand things and can often take longer to do so.

3. I learn best through experimentation, seeing things breakdown, putting it back together and investigating each part. I do believe that "learning styles" is a debunked myth but learning by experience is best for me.
