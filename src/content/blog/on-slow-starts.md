## Slow Starts

Something that Micahel Seibel talked about and I just noticed with Pi is that companies that get to their series A, the best ones were still figuring it out. Coinbase was not an obvious winner and lots of investors will look at the companies in their series A/B/C whatever and in realiaty these companies are peaking. Almost like peaking in high school, the best ones never peak. It's always a rise but the best rises are often slow. Look at any great company (that is the reason you are starting a company right?), they start slow and take a lifetime, even the minimum is 10 years. A decade maybe if you compress lots and lots of time. Nowadays it's looking much longer. Something that Sam Altman said in a talk to YC years ago was that being focused on a long term vision was the only edge left. Everyone is looking for short term growth plays, the only ones that work are long-term aligned plays like building what users want.

If you think about the most long term living organization, best case it would something where the user worked on it their entire life and it just got slowly better and better before hitting critical mass and growing better and better. Like IKEA. He started it when he was 19 and worked on it until he died. IKEA is an instituion and is a masterclass in building a lasting place. Same with Berkshire.


Same with Pi coding agent. The adoption was much slower than Claude Code but it's much stickier and poeple learn with it and stick to it and eventually realize how much you can do and it just explodes.


I think there is also a feeling when you are doing things the right way. Just a feeling of satisfaction and contentment that even if nobody likes what you do, you will.

There is a satisfaction in simplicity that I love. I think simple beautiful things are great and they often get overshadowed by complex plays by smart people. I'm not smart so I'll stick to simple things that I do for myself.

Read my other article on simplicity to see what I mean. People will pitch their startup very complexly as if that is good when in reality it is a horrible sign, it means multiple things, a. you havent't thought deep enough about it and organized the idea, b. mayeb youd on't know but humans are not able to juggle many ideas at once, if you look at the great ads (Read Ogilvy on Advertising), they are one line, maybe 2, but often one idea, one association that they want to get across. They want you to remember one idea, not 10 things it could because the best marketers know that they human brain can really only remember one thing. The best way around this is to just make your product a one-liner, simplify it very very hard and that might mean taking away features (probably a good thing), and being very intentional (hm another good thing). Simplicity is good on many fronts, it's an easier sell, there are less variables, easier to explain, easier to understand, easier to work on. All around yummy. 


The best engineers take away code not add it. The best physcisits simplify the field, not add more complexity.
